Quick Tips to Sound More Natural

  • Read your text out loud — if it sounds strange to your ear, it probably needs fixing.
  • Prefer active voice (“I wrote the report”) over passive (“The report was written by me”).
  • Vary your sentence length — mix short punchy sentences with longer ones.
  • Watch common non-native traps: article usage (a/an/the), prepositions, and word order.
  • When in doubt, paste the sentence into one of the tools above and compare the suggestions.

Bonus Resources

Want to go deeper? Here are a few trusted books and free practice ideas:

  • English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy — classic, clear explanations (great for intermediate learners).
  • Practice speaking with language exchange apps like italki or HelloTalk.
  • Watch English content with subtitles and shadow the speakers (repeat after them).
