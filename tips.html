Practical English Tips You Can Use Today

Simple strategies to sound clearer, more professional, and more natural in emails, chats, and meetings.

Affiliate Disclosure: This page may contain affiliate links to tools mentioned in examples.

1) Keep sentences short

Long sentences often create grammar errors. Aim for one idea per sentence.

2) Avoid direct translation

Translate ideas, not words. Use phrase-level checks with AI tools.

3) Use active voice

“We delivered the report” is clearer than “The report was delivered by us.”

4) Check tone before sending

Friendly but direct tone gets better replies in workplace communication.

Common Mistakes

  • “I am agree” → “I agree”
  • “Discuss about” → “Discuss”
  • “Informations” → “Information”
  • “He explain me” → “He explained it to me”

Recommended workflow

Draft naturally → run grammar check → rewrite unclear lines → final tone check.
